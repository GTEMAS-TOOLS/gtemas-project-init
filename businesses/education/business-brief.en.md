# Business Brief - Education

## Industry Overview
Education institutions (K-12, higher education, vocational training, corporate learning) manage the full student lifecycle: admissions, enrollment, course delivery, assessment, certification, and alumni engagement.

Core challenges include low enrollment conversion rates, inconsistent learning outcomes measurement, disconnected student information systems, manual fee collection, and meeting accessibility/accreditation standards.

Digital transformation is driven by hybrid/online learning demand, data-driven pedagogy, and the need for personalized learning pathways that scale across thousands of students.

## Key Pain Points
- Low admissions conversion (inquiry to enrollment typically < 20%)
- Disconnected SIS, LMS, and billing systems causing data re-entry
- Difficulty tracking student progress and intervention triggers
- Manual timetable conflicts taking weeks to resolve
- Fee collection delays and poor financial visibility
- Lack of learning analytics for curriculum improvement

## Target Personas
- **Academic Director / Dean**: Curriculum quality, accreditation, learning outcomes
- **Admissions Officer**: Lead conversion, application processing, scholarship allocation
- **Instructor / Faculty**: Course delivery, grading, student engagement
- **Registrar**: Enrollment, academic records, transcript management
- **Finance / Bursar**: Tuition billing, payment plans, financial aid
- **Student**: Course access, grades, schedule, payments

## Key Performance Indicators
- Enrollment conversion rate (inquiry to enrolled)
- Student retention rate (year-over-year)
- Course completion rate (target: > 85%)
- Student satisfaction score
- Fee collection rate (target: > 95% on time)
- Faculty-to-student ratio
- Time to issue transcript/certificate

## Regulatory & Compliance Landscape
- Student data privacy (FERPA in US, equivalent elsewhere)
- Accreditation standards compliance
- Accessibility requirements (WCAG, ADA)
- Financial aid and scholarship regulations
- Assessment integrity and anti-cheating policies

## Typical Digital Maturity
Most institutions have basic SIS for enrollment and LMS for content delivery, but they operate as separate systems. Learning analytics is aspirational. Admissions is often spreadsheet-driven. Billing integration with academic records is rare.
