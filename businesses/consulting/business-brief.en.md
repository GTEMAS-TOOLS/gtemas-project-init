# Business Brief - Consulting

## Industry Overview
Professional services firms (consulting, legal, accounting, engineering, IT services) deliver expertise-based engagements with billable hours as the primary revenue model. Operations span business development, resource allocation, project delivery, time tracking, and client billing.

Core challenges include maximizing consultant utilization, maintaining healthy project margins, accurate scoping and estimation, knowledge retention across engagements, and managing the sales-to-delivery handoff.

Technology enables firms to improve visibility into profitability by project/client, automate time capture, and leverage institutional knowledge to reduce reinvention across similar engagements.

## Key Pain Points
- Low consultant utilization rates (industry average: 60-70%, target: 80%+)
- Inaccurate project scoping leading to budget overruns
- Manual timesheet entry with low compliance and late submissions
- Knowledge loss when senior staff leave or between similar projects
- Poor visibility into project profitability until billing stage
- Fragmented tools across sales, delivery, and finance

## Target Personas
- **Managing Partner / CEO**: Firm profitability, client relationships, growth strategy
- **Engagement Manager**: Project delivery, client satisfaction, scope management
- **Consultant / Analyst**: Task execution, time tracking, knowledge access
- **Resource Manager**: Staffing, skills matching, bench management
- **Finance / Billing Manager**: Invoicing, collections, revenue recognition
- **Business Development**: Proposals, pipeline, client acquisition

## Key Performance Indicators
- Utilization rate (target: > 80% billable)
- Revenue per consultant
- Project margin (target: > 30%)
- Win rate on proposals
- Average day sales outstanding (DSO target: < 45 days)
- Client satisfaction / NPS
- Employee retention rate

## Regulatory & Compliance Landscape
- Professional liability and insurance requirements
- Industry-specific licensing (legal, accounting, engineering)
- Data confidentiality and client privilege
- Labor law compliance for contract/freelance workers
- Revenue recognition standards (ASC 606)

## Typical Digital Maturity
Most firms use basic project management and email-based collaboration. Time tracking is often in spreadsheets. PSA tools are adopted by larger firms but rare in small/mid-size. Knowledge management is typically file shares with poor searchability.
