# Business Brief - Logistics

## Industry Overview
Logistics companies manage the movement, storage, and delivery of goods across supply chains. Operations include freight forwarding, warehousing, fleet management, last-mile delivery, and customs clearance.

Core challenges are cost optimization (fuel, labor, freight rates), on-time delivery performance, real-time visibility across the shipment lifecycle, and managing diverse carrier networks.

Technology adoption is driven by e-commerce growth demanding faster deliveries, IoT-enabled fleet tracking, and increasing demand for end-to-end supply chain transparency.

## Key Pain Points
- Lack of real-time shipment visibility leading to customer complaints
- Manual route planning causing 15-25% excess mileage
- Freight invoice discrepancies requiring manual reconciliation
- High driver turnover and difficulty managing compliance documentation
- Disconnected TMS, WMS, and fleet systems creating data silos
- Customs delays due to incomplete or incorrect documentation

## Target Personas
- **Operations Director**: OTIF rates, cost per shipment, network optimization
- **Dispatch Manager**: Load planning, carrier assignment, real-time tracking
- **Warehouse Manager**: Throughput, accuracy, space utilization
- **Fleet Manager**: Vehicle uptime, fuel costs, driver compliance
- **Driver**: Route navigation, proof of delivery, schedule adherence
- **Customer / Shipper**: Shipment tracking, delivery ETAs, documentation

## Key Performance Indicators
- On-time in-full (OTIF) rate (target: > 95%)
- Cost per shipment / cost per mile
- Warehouse throughput (units/hour)
- Fleet utilization rate (target: > 80%)
- Fuel cost per km
- Order accuracy rate (target: > 99%)
- Average delivery lead time

## Regulatory & Compliance Landscape
- Transport safety and driver hour regulations (DOT/HOS)
- Customs and trade compliance (import/export)
- Hazardous materials handling (ADR/IMDG)
- Temperature-controlled logistics (GDP for pharma)
- Insurance and liability requirements

## Typical Digital Maturity
Most logistics companies have basic TMS for shipment booking but lack real-time GPS tracking integration. WMS adoption varies widely. Last-mile visibility is often limited to carrier-provided tracking links. Route optimization is manual or spreadsheet-based.
