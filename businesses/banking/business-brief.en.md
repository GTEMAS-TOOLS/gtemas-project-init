# Business Brief - Banking

## Industry Overview
Financial services and insurance companies operate under strict regulatory oversight, managing customer onboarding (KYC/AML), policy/loan origination, claims processing, risk assessment, and compliance reporting.

Core challenges include lengthy onboarding processes, high manual effort in claims adjudication, regulatory reporting burden, fraud detection, and balancing customer experience with risk controls.

Digital transformation is driven by fintech competition, customer demand for instant approvals, open banking APIs, and regtech solutions for automated compliance.

## Key Pain Points
- Customer onboarding taking days/weeks due to manual KYC checks
- High claims processing costs from manual document review
- Regulatory reporting consuming significant staff time
- Fraud losses from reactive rather than proactive detection
- Legacy core systems limiting product innovation speed
- Poor customer self-service leading to high call volume

## Target Personas
- **Chief Risk Officer**: Risk exposure, regulatory compliance, capital adequacy
- **Branch/Agency Manager**: Sales targets, customer acquisition, service quality
- **Underwriter**: Risk assessment, policy terms, approval speed
- **Claims Adjuster**: Case investigation, settlement accuracy, turnaround time
- **Compliance Officer**: Regulatory reporting, audit preparation, AML monitoring
- **Customer**: Application status, claims tracking, self-service

## Key Performance Indicators
- Customer onboarding time (target: < 24 hours for digital)
- Claims settlement turnaround time
- Loss ratio (claims paid vs. premiums)
- Regulatory compliance score (zero violations)
- Fraud detection rate and false positive rate
- Customer retention rate
- Net interest margin (for lending)

## Regulatory & Compliance Landscape
- KYC/AML regulations (Anti-Money Laundering)
- Insurance regulatory reporting (Solvency II, NAIC)
- Consumer protection and fair lending laws
- Data protection (GDPR, CCPA)
- Capital adequacy requirements (Basel III for banking)

## Typical Digital Maturity
Most financial institutions have core banking/insurance systems but they are legacy mainframes. Digital channels exist but are often thin wrappers over manual processes. Risk and compliance functions rely heavily on spreadsheets. AI adoption for fraud detection is growing but not mainstream.
