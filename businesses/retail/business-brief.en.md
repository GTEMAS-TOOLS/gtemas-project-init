# Business Brief - Retail

## Industry Overview
Retail businesses operate across physical stores, e-commerce, and omnichannel models. Key challenges include real-time inventory visibility, margin optimization, customer retention, and seamless cross-channel experiences.

Core operations span point-of-sale transactions, inventory replenishment, order fulfillment (ship-from-store, click-and-collect), loyalty programs, pricing/promotions, and customer service. Most retailers juggle 8-12 systems that were never designed to work together.

Digital transformation is driven by consumer demand for instant gratification, personalized experiences, and unified commerce across web, mobile, and in-store touchpoints.

## Key Pain Points
- Inventory discrepancies between POS, warehouse, and e-commerce (typically 20-30% inaccuracy)
- Disconnected customer data across online and offline channels
- Stockouts on fast-sellers while slow-movers pile up
- Manual pricing and promotion management prone to margin erosion
- No unified view of customer purchase history for personalization
- Returns processing bottlenecks affecting customer satisfaction

## Target Personas
- **Retail Owner / CEO**: Revenue growth, margin optimization, brand strategy
- **Store Manager**: Daily operations, staff scheduling, sales targets
- **Inventory / Supply Chain Manager**: Stock levels, replenishment, supplier coordination
- **E-commerce Manager**: Online sales, conversion optimization, digital marketing
- **Cashier / Store Associate**: Checkout, customer assistance, returns
- **Customer**: Product discovery, purchase, returns, loyalty rewards

## Key Performance Indicators
- Same-store sales growth (YoY %)
- Inventory turnover ratio (target: 8-12x/year)
- Gross margin return on investment (GMROI)
- Customer retention rate (target: > 60%)
- Average order value (AOV) and basket size
- Stockout rate (target: < 2%)
- E-commerce conversion rate (target: 2-4%)
- Net Promoter Score (NPS > 40)

## Regulatory & Compliance Landscape
- PCI-DSS for payment card processing
- Consumer protection and return policies
- Sales tax / VAT compliance across jurisdictions
- GDPR / privacy laws for customer data
- Product safety and labeling regulations

## Typical Digital Maturity
Most retailers have basic POS and accounting but lack real-time inventory sync across channels. E-commerce is often a separate silo. Loyalty programs exist but rarely connect to purchase data for personalization. Supply chain visibility is limited to Tier-1 suppliers.
