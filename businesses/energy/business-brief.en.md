# Business Brief - Energy

## Industry Overview
Energy companies manage power generation, transmission, distribution, and retail across fossil, nuclear, and renewable sources. Operations span grid management, asset maintenance, regulatory compliance, and customer billing.

Digital transformation focuses on smart grid technology, predictive maintenance, demand response, distributed energy resources, and decarbonization tracking.

Key challenges include aging infrastructure, integrating renewables, regulatory compliance across jurisdictions, outage management, and customer engagement for energy efficiency.

## Key Pain Points
- Aging grid infrastructure requiring $billions in modernization
- Integrating intermittent renewable sources into grid stability
- Complex regulatory compliance across multiple jurisdictions
- High cost of unplanned outages ($1M+ per hour for large utilities)
- Customer demand for real-time usage data and green energy options
- Cybersecurity threats to critical infrastructure

## Target Personas
- **Grid Operations Manager**: Grid stability, load balancing, outage response
- **Plant Manager**: Generation efficiency, maintenance, safety
- **Regulatory Compliance Officer**: Reporting, tariff filings, environmental compliance
- **Customer Service Manager**: Billing, outage communication, energy programs
- **Asset Manager**: Capital planning, equipment lifecycle, investment decisions

## Key Performance Indicators
- System Average Interruption Duration Index (SAIDI)
- Generation availability factor (target: >92%)
- Renewable energy percentage of total generation
- Customer satisfaction score (J.D. Power)
- O&M cost per MWh
- Safety incident rate (TRIR)

## Regulatory & Compliance Landscape
- NERC reliability standards
- EPA environmental regulations
- State/federal rate-setting and tariff regulations
- Renewable portfolio standards (RPS)
- Critical infrastructure protection (CIP) cybersecurity

## Typical Digital Maturity
Utilities have adopted SCADA and EMS but face integration challenges with newer DER, smart meter, and IoT platforms. Customer-facing digital channels lag behind other industries.
