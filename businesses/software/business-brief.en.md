# Business Brief - Software

## Industry Overview
IT outsourcing and solutions companies provide software development, infrastructure management, and technology consulting services to clients across industries. Engagement models include staff augmentation, dedicated teams, project-based delivery, and managed services.

Core challenges include talent acquisition and retention in competitive markets, maintaining consistent delivery quality across distributed teams, accurate project estimation, client satisfaction management, and balancing bench costs with growth.

Technology adoption focuses on DevOps and CI/CD automation, project delivery platforms, talent management systems, and knowledge management to reduce ramp-up time and improve delivery consistency across engagements.

## Key Pain Points
- High engineer turnover (industry average: 15-25% annually)
- Talent bench costs eating into margins when between projects
- Inconsistent delivery quality across teams and projects
- Poor visibility into project health until escalation
- Knowledge loss during team transitions and member departures
- Client communication gaps leading to scope creep and dissatisfaction

## Target Personas
- **CEO / Managing Director**: Revenue growth, margins, client portfolio, talent strategy
- **Delivery Manager / PMO**: Project delivery, quality, risk, client satisfaction
- **Engineering Manager / Tech Lead**: Team productivity, code quality, architecture decisions
- **HR / Talent Acquisition**: Recruitment pipeline, retention, skills development
- **Software Engineer**: Development tasks, career growth, tooling, knowledge access
- **Client / Product Owner**: Progress visibility, deliverable quality, communication
- **Business Development / Sales**: Pipeline, proposals, deal closure, account growth

## Key Performance Indicators
- Billable utilization rate (target: > 80%)
- Revenue per engineer
- Employee attrition rate (target: < 15%)
- Client satisfaction score (CSAT / NPS)
- Project on-time delivery rate (target: > 90%)
- Sprint velocity consistency (variation < 15%)
- Defect escape rate to production
- Average time to fill open positions (target: < 30 days)

## Regulatory & Compliance Landscape
- Data protection and confidentiality (NDA, GDPR, SOC2)
- Intellectual property rights and work product ownership
- Labor law compliance across jurisdictions (for distributed teams)
- Export control regulations for technology work
- Industry-specific compliance of client sectors (HIPAA, PCI-DSS, etc.)

## Typical Digital Maturity
IT outsourcing firms are technically mature but often lack integrated business operations tooling. Project management is Jira/Azure DevOps but disconnected from resource planning and billing. Talent management is basic HRIS without skills tracking. Knowledge management is scattered across wikis and shared drives.
