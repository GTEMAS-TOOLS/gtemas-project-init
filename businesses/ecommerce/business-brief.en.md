# Business Brief - Ecommerce

## Industry Overview
Direct-to-consumer (D2C) e-commerce businesses sell directly to end customers through owned digital channels. Operations span storefront management, order fulfillment, customer acquisition through performance marketing, customer support, and retention through personalization.

Core challenges include customer acquisition cost (CAC) inflation, conversion rate optimization, fulfillment speed expectations (same-day/next-day), return rate management, and building customer lifetime value (LTV) in a competitive landscape.

Technology enables growth through personalized shopping experiences, automated marketing funnels, efficient order orchestration, and data-driven product/pricing decisions.

## Key Pain Points
- Rising customer acquisition costs (CAC increasing 50-70% over 5 years)
- Low conversion rates (industry average: 1.5-3%)
- High return rates (20-40% for fashion)
- Cart abandonment (average: 70%)
- Manual customer support overwhelming small teams
- Difficulty attributing marketing spend to revenue

## Target Personas
- **E-commerce Founder / CEO**: Revenue growth, unit economics, brand building
- **Marketing Manager**: CAC, ROAS, channel mix, campaigns
- **Operations Manager**: Fulfillment speed, returns processing, cost control
- **Customer Support Manager**: Ticket volume, resolution time, CSAT
- **Product Manager**: Catalog, merchandising, conversion optimization
- **Customer**: Product discovery, checkout, delivery, returns

## Key Performance Indicators
- Conversion rate (target: 2-4%)
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
- Customer lifetime value (LTV) and LTV:CAC ratio (target: > 3:1)
- Average order value (AOV)
- Return rate (target improvement)
- Cart abandonment rate (target: < 65%)
- Net Promoter Score (NPS)

## Regulatory & Compliance Landscape
- Consumer protection and return policies
- PCI-DSS for payment processing
- GDPR / privacy laws for customer data
- Product safety and labeling
- Advertising and marketing compliance

## Typical Digital Maturity
D2C businesses are digital-native but often lack integration between their storefront, OMS, marketing, and CRM tools. Attribution modeling is fragmented. Personalization is basic (segment-level, not individual). Returns processing is frequently manual.
