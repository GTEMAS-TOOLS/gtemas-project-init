# Business Brief - Hospitality

## Industry Overview
Hospitality businesses (hotels, resorts, restaurants, event venues) focus on delivering exceptional guest experiences while maximizing revenue per available room/seat and controlling operational costs.

Core operations include reservations, front desk operations, housekeeping, food & beverage, revenue management, and guest loyalty programs. Most properties juggle 6-10 separate systems that rarely share data.

Technology adoption is driven by OTA competition, guest expectations for mobile-first experiences, and the need for dynamic pricing to optimize yield across seasons and demand fluctuations.

## Key Pain Points
- Revenue leakage from static pricing and OTA commission dependency
- Disconnected PMS, POS, and channel manager causing overbookings
- Housekeeping delays impacting room turnover and guest satisfaction
- No unified guest profile across stays for personalization
- High F&B waste from poor demand forecasting
- Manual guest communication (pre-arrival, in-stay, post-stay)

## Target Personas
- **General Manager**: RevPAR, guest satisfaction, operational efficiency
- **Front Desk Agent**: Check-in/out, guest requests, room assignments
- **Revenue Manager**: Pricing strategy, demand forecasting, channel mix
- **Housekeeping Supervisor**: Room turnovers, task assignment, quality checks
- **F&B Manager**: Menu management, inventory, service flow
- **Guest**: Booking, check-in, requests, loyalty rewards

## Key Performance Indicators
- Revenue per available room (RevPAR)
- Average daily rate (ADR)
- Occupancy rate (target: > 70%)
- Guest satisfaction score (target: > 4.5/5)
- OTA vs. direct booking ratio
- Housekeeping room turnover time (target: < 30 min)
- Food cost percentage (target: 28-35%)

## Regulatory & Compliance Landscape
- Fire safety and building codes for hospitality
- Food safety and hygiene standards (HACCP)
- Guest data privacy (GDPR/local laws)
- Liquor licensing and service regulations
- Accessibility requirements (ADA)

## Typical Digital Maturity
Most properties have a PMS for reservations and a separate POS for F&B, but they operate independently. Channel manager adoption varies. Revenue management is often manual or basic rule-based. Guest loyalty programs exist but rarely leverage data for personalization.

## App Catalog (10 apps)

### Essential (build first)
1. **pms** — Property management system for reservations, check-in/out, room inventory, and folio billing
2. **restaurant-pos** — Restaurant POS for table orders, kitchen display, payments, and shift management

### Important (phase 2)
1. **booking-engine** — Direct booking engine for website with availability, pricing, promotions, and upselling
2. **housekeeping-operations** — Room status management, task assignment, maintenance requests, and quality inspections
3. **revenue-management** — Dynamic pricing, demand forecasting, and channel strategy optimization
4. **crm** — Customer relationship management for hospitality with lead tracking, pipeline, and engagement
5. **accounting-finance** — General ledger, AP/AR, financial reporting, and budgeting for hospitality
6. **hris** — HR management with employee profiles, payroll, attendance, and performance for hospitality
7. **bi-analytics** — KPI dashboards, self-service reporting, and data analytics for hospitality

### Advanced (scale & optimization)
1. **guest-loyalty** — Loyalty program with member profiles, points, personalized offers, and engagement campaigns
