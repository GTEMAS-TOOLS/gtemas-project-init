# Business Brief - Real Estate

## Industry Overview
Real estate businesses manage property portfolios spanning commercial, residential, and mixed-use assets. Operations include property acquisition, tenant management, lease administration, facility maintenance, and asset valuation.

Core challenges include maximizing occupancy rates, efficient lease lifecycle management, timely maintenance response, accurate property valuation, and managing broker/agent relationships for sales.

Technology adoption is driven by PropTech innovation, smart building IoT integration, virtual property tours, and data-driven investment decisions.

## Key Pain Points
- Manual lease tracking causing missed renewals and escalation dates
- Slow maintenance response times damaging tenant satisfaction
- Disconnected broker CRM, property management, and accounting systems
- Inaccurate property valuations due to outdated market data
- No centralized view of portfolio performance across properties
- High tenant turnover from reactive rather than proactive management

## Target Personas
- **Portfolio Manager / Owner**: NOI, occupancy rates, asset appreciation
- **Property Manager**: Tenant satisfaction, maintenance, rent collection
- **Leasing Agent / Broker**: Lead conversion, deal pipeline, commissions
- **Facility Manager**: Maintenance coordination, vendor management, SLAs
- **Tenant**: Service requests, payments, communication
- **Finance Controller**: Rent roll, CAM reconciliation, financial reporting

## Key Performance Indicators
- Occupancy rate (target: > 90%)
- Net operating income (NOI) growth
- Tenant retention rate (target: > 80%)
- Average time to lease (vacant to occupied)
- Maintenance response time (target: < 24h for non-emergency)
- Rent collection rate (target: > 98%)

## Regulatory & Compliance Landscape
- Lease and tenancy laws
- Fair housing regulations
- Building safety and fire codes
- Environmental and energy efficiency standards
- Real estate transaction and disclosure requirements

## Typical Digital Maturity
Most real estate companies use basic property management software for rent collection but lack integrated lease management. Broker CRMs are often standalone tools. Facility maintenance is frequently phone/email-based. Portfolio analytics is spreadsheet-driven.
