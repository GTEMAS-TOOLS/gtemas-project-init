# Business Brief - Construction

## Industry Overview
Construction companies manage complex, multi-stakeholder projects spanning bidding, design coordination, procurement, field execution, quality assurance, and handover. Projects range from residential buildings to large infrastructure.

Core challenges include cost overruns (industry average: 80% of projects exceed budget), schedule delays, safety incident management, change order disputes, and coordination across dozens of subcontractors.

Digital adoption is accelerating through BIM, drone surveys, IoT-enabled equipment tracking, and mobile-first field applications, but many firms still rely on spreadsheets and email for project controls.

## Key Pain Points
- Cost overruns due to poor change order tracking and scope creep
- Schedule delays from subcontractor coordination failures
- Safety incidents and compliance violations on job sites
- Paper-based daily reports and inspection checklists
- Disconnected estimating, project management, and accounting systems
- Difficulty tracking equipment utilization across multiple sites

## Target Personas
- **Project Director / PM**: Budget, schedule, risk, stakeholder communication
- **Site Superintendent**: Daily operations, subcontractor coordination, safety
- **Estimator**: Bid preparation, quantity takeoff, cost databases
- **Procurement Manager**: Vendor selection, PO management, material delivery
- **Safety Officer**: Incident tracking, training, compliance inspections
- **Field Worker / Foreman**: Task execution, timesheets, issue reporting

## Key Performance Indicators
- Project cost variance (target: < 5% over budget)
- Schedule performance index (SPI target: > 0.95)
- Safety incident rate (TRIR target: < 2.0)
- RFI response time (target: < 48 hours)
- Change order processing time
- Subcontractor on-time delivery rate

## Regulatory & Compliance Landscape
- OSHA / local occupational health and safety standards
- Building codes and permits
- Environmental impact and waste management regulations
- Labor and subcontractor compliance
- Insurance and bonding requirements

## Typical Digital Maturity
Most construction firms use basic project management and accounting software but lack integrated project controls. Field data collection is predominantly paper-based. BIM adoption is growing for large projects but rare for small/mid firms.
