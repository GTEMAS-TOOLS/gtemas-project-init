# Business Brief - Manufacturing

## Industry Overview
Manufacturing enterprises manage complex production workflows spanning raw material procurement, production planning, shopfloor execution, quality control, equipment maintenance, and finished goods distribution.

Key challenges include minimizing unplanned downtime, improving Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE), maintaining product traceability for recalls, and managing multi-tier supply chains with volatile lead times.

Industry 4.0 adoption is accelerating with IoT sensors, digital twins, and AI-driven predictive maintenance, but most mid-size manufacturers still rely on spreadsheets and disconnected legacy systems for production planning.

## Key Pain Points
- Unplanned equipment downtime causing 5-20% capacity loss
- Manual production scheduling with no real-time shopfloor visibility
- Quality escapes due to late detection of non-conformances
- Lack of end-to-end traceability for regulatory recalls
- Disconnected MES, ERP, and maintenance systems
- Supplier delivery variability disrupting production plans

## Target Personas
- **Plant Manager**: Production output, OEE, cost per unit
- **Production Planner**: Scheduling, capacity balance, material availability
- **Quality Manager**: Defect rates, CAPA, audit readiness
- **Maintenance Engineer**: Equipment uptime, preventive maintenance, spare parts
- **Shopfloor Operator**: Work order execution, quality checks, machine operation
- **Supply Chain Manager**: Procurement, supplier performance, inventory optimization

## Key Performance Indicators
- Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE target: > 85%)
- First Pass Yield (target: > 95%)
- Unplanned downtime hours per month
- On-time delivery rate (target: > 95%)
- Cost per unit produced
- Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF)
- Inventory turns (target: 8-12x/year)

## Regulatory & Compliance Landscape
- ISO 9001 quality management
- ISO 14001 environmental management
- OSHA / local occupational safety standards
- Product-specific regulations (FDA for pharma/food, CE marking, etc.)
- Supply chain traceability and recall readiness

## Typical Digital Maturity
Most manufacturers have ERP for finance and procurement but lack real-time shopfloor data. MES adoption is growing but often limited to a single production line. Predictive maintenance is aspirational for most; reactive maintenance dominates.
